# Customer-Interaction-Analysis-in-Online-Retail-Store

This project analyzes customer interactions with an online retail store using the Software Metrics approach. The goal is to understand customer behavior, engagement, satisfaction, and churn by applying the Goal–Question–Metric (GQM) framework.

---

## Project Overview

Customer retention is critical for the success of online retail businesses. Understanding why customers stop purchasing (churn) helps organizations design effective marketing and retention strategies.

This project uses customer demographic, behavioral, and engagement data to analyze churn patterns and derive actionable insights through well-defined software metrics.

---

## Objective

- Analyze customer demographics and spending behavior  
- Evaluate customer engagement and satisfaction  
- Monitor customer activity and churn  
- Assess the effectiveness of marketing campaigns  
- Apply the GQM framework for structured metric analysis  

---

## Dataset Description

Source: Kaggle – Online Retail Customer Churn Dataset  

### Key Attributes
- Customer_ID  
- Age  
- Gender  
- Annual_Income  
- Total_Spend  
- Years_as_Customer  
- Num_of_Purchases  
- Average_Transaction_Amount  
- Num_of_Returns  
- Num_of_Support_Contacts  
- Satisfaction_Score (1–5)  
- Last_Purchase_Days_Ago  
- Email_Opt_In  
- Promotion_Response  
- Target_Churn (0 – No churn, 1 – Churn)

---

## Methodology

### GQM Framework
**Goal:** Understand customer behavior and churn  
**Questions:**  
- How does age and gender affect customer behavior?  
- How does spending correlate with income?  
- How does customer support interaction impact satisfaction?  
- What factors influence customer churn?  

**Metrics Used:**  
- Mean, Median, Standard Deviation  
- Correlation Coefficient  
- Quartiles and Distributions  
- Churn Rate  
- Response Rate to Promotions  

---

## Analysis Performed

- Customer demographic analysis  
- Spending behavior and income correlation  
- Customer engagement analysis  
- Satisfaction score distribution  
- Support interaction vs satisfaction  
- Email opt-in analysis  
- Promotion response analysis  
- Churn rate analysis  

---

## Results and Insights

- Churn rate is higher than customer retention rate  
- Satisfaction scores mostly fall in the mid-range (average satisfaction)  
- Weak correlation between support contacts and satisfaction  
- Less than half of customers respond to promotional campaigns  
- About half of customers opted in for email communication
